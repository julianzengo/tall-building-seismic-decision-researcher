The distinction

A researcher is not a summarizer.

A summarizer condenses what's in front of it. A researcher asks what's missing, challenges the framing, and weighs sources by credibility before it concludes anything.

✕ A Summarizer

  • Produces an answer immediately
  • Describes the system you named
  • Treats every source as equal
  • Proceeds on unstated assumptions
  • Presents one option as the answer
  • Sounds more certain than the evidence

✓ This Researcher

  • Asks what decision you're making first
  • Names what information is missing
  • Weighs EC8 vs. PEER vs. a sales brochure differently
  • Surfaces and challenges each assumption
  • Develops competing alternatives
  • States confidence: High / Moderate / Low
Three things a chatbot won't do

Built to investigate, not inform.

/ 01

Inventories what's missing

Before evaluating any system, it lists the unknowns — seismic hazard, ground type, regularity, ductility class, contractor capability — and won't conclude until the material gaps are named.

/ 02

Challenges the load-bearing assumption

Is wind actually secondary to seismic? Is that behavior factor achievable with this contractor? It names the assumption, says what it affects, and asks what would verify it.

/ 03

Weighs sources by credibility

A National Annex, a PEER report, and a manufacturer's design guide are not equal evidence. It tells you which tier a claim rests on — and flags when sources conflict.

The workflow

Five steps, every investigation.

Define the decision

System selection? Foundation type? Ductility class? Performance target? It pins down what's actually being decided, who's deciding, and at what project stage — before researching anything.

Identify missing information

Seismic hazard, site conditions, soil class, regularity, height and aspect ratio. The gaps that would change the conclusion are surfaced explicitly.

Develop competing hypotheses

At least two or three live alternatives — never a single option evaluated in isolation — with the conditions that would favor each.

Evaluate the evidence

Code requirements, research findings, professional guidance, and comparable precedents — each weighed by its source tier.

State confidence and uncertainty

What's known, what remains uncertain, what additional information is needed, and what the consequence of acting early would be.
