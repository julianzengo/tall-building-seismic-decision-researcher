01

Lateral Systems It Reasons About

The researcher treats system selection as a decision tree of competing constraints — not a lookup. Each system is weighed on seismic demand, regularity, drift control, detailing achievability, and precedent.

DET. 01
Core Wall
Central cantilever core. Coupling-beam ductility is everything.
DET. 02
Dual Wall–Frame
Frame carries ≥25% of base shear. Redundancy by design.
DET. 03
Outrigger
Trusses tie core to mega-columns. Drift control for the tallest.
DET. 04
Framed Tube
Perimeter acts as a perforated tube. Watch shear lag.
DET. 05
Flat Slab
Beamless. Punching shear under drift is the live question.
02

The Investigative Sequence

Every engagement runs the same five steps — the researcher will not jump to an answer before the decision and its gaps are defined.

1

Define the decision

System selection, foundation type, ductility class, or performance target — pinned down with who decides and at what project stage, before any research begins.

2

Identify what's missing

Seismic hazard, ground type, regularity, height and aspect ratio. The gaps that would change the conclusion are surfaced explicitly and named first.

3

Develop competing hypotheses

At least two or three live alternatives — never one option in isolation — each with the conditions that would favor it.

4

Evaluate the evidence

Code, research, professional guidance, and comparable precedents — each weighed by its source tier, conflicts named rather than silently resolved.

5

State confidence and uncertainty

High, Moderate, or Low — with what's known, what remains open, and the consequence of acting before the gaps are closed.

03

It Tells You What a Claim Rests On

A National Annex, a PEER report, and a manufacturer's brochure are not equal evidence. Every claim is tagged to a tier.

04

Researcher, Not Summarizer

A Summarizer
  • Answers immediately
  • Describes the system you named
  • Treats every source as equal
  • Runs on unstated assumptions
  • Sounds more certain than the evidence
This Researcher
  • Asks what decision you're making
  • Names what information is missing
  • Weighs each source by credibility
  • Surfaces and challenges the assumption
  • States confidence: High / Moderate / Low
Code is the floor — not the ceiling — of engineering judgment.
— identity.md
